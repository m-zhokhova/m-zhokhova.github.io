About me

Most data scientists pick a lane. I've spent 4+ years as an independent consultant shipping production ML across industries — pharmaceutical supply chains, retail forecasting, genomics — before going back to push the research side further.

"The interesting question isn't whether a model works. It's whether it still works when the data stops cooperating."

Right now I'm doing something most ML engineers haven't touched: applying Graph Neural Networks to biological networks to predict which genes drive disease. It's one of the rarer intersections in the field, and it turns out everything I know about messy production data applies just as well to messy genomic data.

I write on Medium about what I actually learn — including the approaches that didn't work, which tend to be more useful than the ones that did.

Disease Gene Prediction via Graph Neural Networks

MSc thesis. A pipeline that identifies disease-associated genes inside human Gene Regulatory Networks — combining GNN-based deep learning with Louvain community detection, centrality analysis, and data from TRRUST and DisGeNET. The goal: make sense of which genes matter, and why, at the network level.
